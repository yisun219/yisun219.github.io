## Research Experience

**High-Performance Heterogeneous ASIC Accelerator for Transformer Models**
*Hong Kong University of Science and Technology (HKUST)*
*Supervised by Prof. Tim CHENG and Prof. Chi-Ying TSUI | 2025.10 - Present*
- **Architect** a heterogeneous multi-core ASIC dedicated to accelerating Transformer-based neural networks.
- **Design** a specialized hardware accelerator integrating heterogeneous processing units to optimize the execution of Transformer workloads.
- **Implement** a high-performance hardware solution leveraging heterogeneous cores to effectively balance computation and memory access.

**Generation and Optimization of Spatial Accelerators for Tensor Workloads**
*Shanghai Jiao Tong University (SJTU)*
*Supervised by Prof. Jieru Zhao | 2025.09 - 2025.12*
- **Implement** an Output Stationary (OS) Systolic Array using Verilog for high-performance computing tasks.
- **Deploy** and **reproduce** the **Gemmini** framework to explore hardware-software co-design for accelerating matrix multiplication and deep learning workloads.
- **Propose** a novel mapping strategy and optimized dataflow, resulting in significantly improved data reuse and hardware utilization.

**TCN-Enhanced Transformer for Wideband Digital Predistortion**
*South China University of Technology*
*Supervised by Prof. Xiang Yi and Prof. Enyi Yao | 2025.03 - 2025.09*
- **Propose** a novel neural network algorithm based on Temporal Convolutional Networks (TCN) and Transformers for digital predistortion, applicable to both Digital Power Amplifiers (DPA) and Analog Power Amplifiers (APA).
- **Leverage** the OpenDPD training framework to implement the digital pre-distortion algorithm, achieving training performance that exceeds baseline models.

**Millimeter Wave Reconfigurable Low Noise Amplifier Design (65nm CMOS)**
*South China University of Technology*
*Supervised by Prof. Li Gao | 2024.11 - 2025.10*
- **Design** a millimeter-wave Low Noise Amplifier (LNA) with band-reconfigurable functions using TSMC 65nm CMOS process, achieving optimal S-parameters, low power consumption, and low noise factor.
- **Explore** novel reconfigurable inter-stage and output matching networks, minimizing Q-factor loss during functional reconfiguration.
- **Conduct** design and simulation of tri-coupled transformers within the matching networks.

**Analog Front End Design for High-speed Serial Interface Receiver**
*South China University of Technology*
*Supervised by Prof. Guangyin Feng | 2025.03 - 2025.08*
- **Design** and **implement** a 100Gb/s PAM-4 Analog Front End (AFE) in TSMC 65nm technology, integrating a 1-stage CTLE and a 2-stage VGA.
- **Demonstrate** 3-12dB peaking gain at the Nyquist frequency through post-layout simulation, ensuring signal integrity for high-speed transmission.
- **Extend** system bandwidth by utilizing a transformer load in the first-stage VGA to introduce an auxiliary pole, while optimizing total power consumption to ~11mW.

**Receiver Design Based on Kramers-Kronig Relation**
*South China University of Technology*
*Supervised by Prof. Guangyin Feng | 2024.09 - 2024.11*
- **Conduct** system-level modeling of a Kramers-Kronig (KK) receiver in MATLAB, reproducing the architecture of a synthesizer-free coherent receiver (Ref: ISSCC 12.2).
- **Investigate** mixed-signal phase reconstruction techniques and **develop** a compensation algorithm to effectively mitigate phase signal distortion.
